## Teaching Philosophy

<div class="philosophy-section">
  <div class="philosophy-card">
    <div class="philosophy-icon">
      <i class="fas fa-hands-helping"></i>
    </div>
    <h3>Interactive Learning</h3>
    <p>I believe learning is most effective when students actively engage with material through hands-on exercises, coding challenges, and real-world examples. I encourage questions and foster a classroom environment where curiosity is celebrated.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="philosophy-card">
    <div class="philosophy-icon">
      <i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
    </div>
    <h3>Concept Visualization</h3>
    <p>Complex algorithms and data structures become clearer through visual representations. I use diagrams, animations, and live coding demonstrations to help students build mental models of computational processes.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="philosophy-card">
    <div class="philosophy-icon">
      <i class="fas fa-layer-group"></i>
    </div>
    <h3>Scaffolded Learning</h3>
    <p>I structure material to build progressively from fundamentals to advanced topics, ensuring students have solid foundations before tackling complex problems. Each concept serves as a building block for the next.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="philosophy-card">
    <div class="philosophy-icon">
      <i class="fas fa-users"></i>
    </div>
    <h3>Collaborative Problem-Solving</h3>
    <p>Group projects and peer programming sessions teach students to communicate technical concepts and work effectively in teamsâ€”skills essential for success in research and industry.</p>
  </div>
</div>
